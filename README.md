Introduction
SEAMAN is an innovative climate data visualization tool that translates NASA-Power data (1984-2022) into an interactive digital seascape. Designed to enhance climate change communication, SEAMAN transforms complex climate data into an intuitive, visually symbolic experience for educators, researchers, policymakers, and broader audiences. With each element—the sea, moon, stars, and sky—rooted in our cultural imagination, SEAMAN enables users to explore temperature and precipitation anomalies and comprehend the broader implications of global climate trends.

Features
🌍 Global Scope: Retrieve and visualize climate data for any location worldwide.
🌡️ Temperature Trends: Represented by waves’ color, movement, and sound.
🌕 Precipitation Patterns: Depicted through the moon's size and position.
⭐ Aridity Indicators: Conveyed by a constellation of stars.
🎧 Interactive Soundscape: Dynamic, evolving auditory elements linked to temperature anomalies.
🖥️ Visual & Auditory Metaphors: Transform climate data into an immersive, multi-sensory experience.

Key Components
🌊 The Sea
Represents: Air temperature trends.
Visuals: Wave color and movement shift in response to temperature anomalies.
Sound: Waves produce sounds, with frequency and rhythm tied to thermal deviations.
🌕 The Moon
Represents: Monthly and annual precipitation.
Visuals: The size and vertical position of the moon shift with rainfall patterns, with greener hues indicating higher rates.
⭐ The Stars
Represents: Aridity levels.
Visuals: The stars form constellations that remain largely static, but their brightness varies according to extreme precipitation deficits or surpluses.
🎧 Soundscape
Dynamic Elements: Synth loops, drums, and distorted melodies mirror temperature anomalies, with faster, more intense beats accompanying increased climatic instability.
Customizations
Modify the experience to fit your needs:

Adjust the visual style by editing the parameters in seaman_config.json.
Customize the soundscapes by replacing audio loops in the /sounds directory.
Explore alternative data sources by linking new datasets into the data/ folder.
